Abstract

Space propulsion thrusters are core elements of any spacecraft that requires mobility. Electric propulsion covers a particular niche by utilising electrical energy for thrust creation. In this study, an experimental characterisation of the XR-100 resistojet is conducted at the European Space Agency (ESA) Propulsion Laboratory (EPL). The work documents the experimental setup, thrust balance operation, uncertainty analysis, and comparison between measured performance and analytical modelling under cold-flow and heated conditions.
